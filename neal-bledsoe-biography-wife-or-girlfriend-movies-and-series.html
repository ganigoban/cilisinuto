Neal Bledsoe – biography

Neal Bledsoe is a Canadian-American actor and author born in Toronto, Ontario, Canada on March 26, 1981 and raised in Seattle, Washington. If you’re wondering if the actor is in any way related to Drew Bledsoe, the retired New England Patriots quarterback of the National Football League, yes, he’s his second cousin.

Neal grew up moving around Seattle a lot. He has lived in places such as Queen Anne, Magnolia, Ballard, Ravenna, Edmonds, Downtown, West Seattle and Fremont at various times. He began his education at Roosevelt High School, continued his education at Garfield High School, completed the CEDU school system, and graduated from Shawnigan Lake School.

He then went to the Idyllwild Arts Academy, where he studied acting and writing for a year. After graduating from art school, Neal Bledsoe continued his artistic training at the North Carolina School of the Arts under the tutelage of veteran theater director Gerald Freedman. He graduated in 2005 and earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts degree.

Movies and TV Shows

After graduating, Neal Bledsoe got off to a flying start in his acting career. He achieved his first professional acting maturity in 2 episodes of the television series Exemplar (2005), as the character Quinn less than a month after graduating from high school. That same year, Neal played the character of Noah in The Ridge Other film works in which he has starred include Revolutionary Road (2008), Lost and Under New Direction both in 2009, Sex and the City 2 (2010), The Walken Dead, High-rise, and A Kiss for Jed All In 2011

He continued to build his acting portfolio, playing various characters in Crossroads (2012), You Over There? (2013), Grand Street and West End both in 2014, Primary (2015), After the Sun Has Fallen (2016) and Police State (2017) where he appeared alongside Sean Young, Seth Gilliam and others. Bledsoe also played the character of Frank in Soldier ‘s Heart (2019).

On television here he has been much more successful and busy with roles in projects such as CSI: NY (2007) in the episode “… Come Over” Law & Order: Criminal Intent (2008) in the episode: “Please note that we no more accepting letters of recommendation from Henry Kissinger.” Neal Bledsoe’s acting breakthrough came in Gossip Girl (2009), playing the character of Josh Elis in the episode “Enough About Eve,” and another spectacular portrayal of the character Tyler Meade-Hartley in 7 episodes of Ugly Betty that ran from 2009 to 2010.

After that, he scored more acting roles in TV series such as Body of Evidence (2011), Episode: “Society Hill”, Smash (2012) in which he appeared as John Goodwin in 7 episodes. Next came The Secrets of Laura (2015-2016) as Tony Abbott in 11 episodes, The Man in the High Castle as Captain Connolly in 4 episodes alongside Carsten Norgaard, Rick Worthy, Amy Okuda, Hiro Kanagawa and others.

Neal was also seen on Timeless (2016) as Robert Todd Lincoln, Coming Home for Christmas as Robert Marley and The Last Tycoon as Axel both in 2017. More recently, the Canadian-American actor acted in Chiefs as Enrizio and Max Whitford in Shameless (2018).

Remember we already mentioned Neal Bledsoe is also an author. In this regard, he is credited with published work in Men’s Health journal by Rashard Mendenhall. In 2015, Neal also wrote the short film Primary, which explored open relationships. He directed and played a leading role in the film.

Who is his girlfriend or wife?
