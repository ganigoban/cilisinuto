Isabella Rossellini’s Early Life and Career

Rossellini was born in Rome, Italy, to Swedish actress Ingrid Bergman and Italian director Roberto Rossellini. She moved to New York at the age of 19, where she worked as a translator and a television reporter before stepping into the world of modeling.

Modeling Career

Rossellini’s modeling career took off when she signed a contract with Lancôme in 1982. She became the highest-paid model in the world, which significantly contributed to her net worth. However, her contract was not renewed in 1996, which Rossellini attributed to the company’s desire for a younger face. In a surprising turn of events, Lancôme rehired her in 2016, acknowledging the need for age diversity in the beauty industry.

Acting Career

Rossellini’s acting career began in 1976, but her breakthrough role came in 1986 with the film “Blue Velvet.” She has since appeared in numerous films and television series, earning critical acclaim and financial success.
