This is how Top Gun star Miles Tellers facial scars came about.

 Posted on August 28, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  359 words  |  Patria Henriques

The American actor Miles Teller with his unique talent as an entertainer, precisely an actor in films like Top Gun: Maverick (2022), Whiplash (2014), Footloose (2011), Fantastic Four (2015), and The Spectacular Now (2013) has won him lots of accolades including nominations in numerous award like BAFTA Rising Star Award, MTV Movie & TV Award, People’s Choice Award, and Young Hollywood Award. 

Who is Miles Teller?

On February 20, 1987, Miles Teller who at present has a grand height of 6 ft 1 inch (1.85m) and a weight of 79 kg (174 lbs) was welcomed by his parents Mike Teller, an engineer and Merry Flowers a real estate agent at Downingtown, Pennsylvania in the United States of America. He started acting at a very young age and even became the president of his high school drama club as well as a fully-fledged sportsman engaged in baseball. He has his high school education at Lecanto High School and graduated from New York University Tisch School of the Arts with a Bachelor of Fine Arts in drama in 2009. In 2010, he started his acting career as a professional landing a major role in Rabbit Hole (2010). Moving forward he starred in a number of movies and was exposed to fame, starring in the films Whiplash, The Spectacular Now, The Divergent Series, and Top Gun: Maverick. 

He’s bagged a number of awards and nominations for his superb acting skills.  

What happened to Miles Teller?

In 2007, the American movie star was involved in a fatal car accident alongside his friends when returning from a music festival. The car lost control at 80 mph (130 km/h) and skidded eight times, throwing him more than 30 feet away from the vehicle. He underwent several painful complicated surgeries to correct some injuries and smoothen his seriously injured face for months, eventually still leaving multiple scars on his face. 

How old was he when the incident happened?

 Miles was 20 years old in 2007 when he was involved in a fatal car accident. He was as well in college at New York University Tisch School of the Arts reading Bachelor of Fine Arts in drama.
