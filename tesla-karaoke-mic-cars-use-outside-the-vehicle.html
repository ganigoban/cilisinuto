Tesla releases karaoke mic for its cars - and you can use it outside the vehicle too

 Posted on September 3, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  366 words  |  Kelle Repass

TESLA is taking carpool karaoke to the next level.

It introduced the TeslaMic which you can use in its cars.

Americans will have to wait to get their hands on the latest novelty, as it's only being sold in China.

The TeslaMic was available at the Chinese Tesla online shop and, reportedly, sold out in an hour.

It comes as a set of microphones for use in Teslas.

It also includes a companion karaoke app called Leishi KTV.

The TeslaMic was selling for $188.48.

Even when your electric vehicle is in drive, karaoke tracks and videos will appear on screen for your passengers to sing-along.

The idea behind the TeslaMic is to turn your Tesla into a private karaoke booth while you're parked.

The microphone can be used outside of Teslas, as well.

It comes with different sound modes, allowing even challenged crooners an opportunity to sound like a rock star.

Tesla recently expanded its US trademark to audio products which could pave the way for the TeslaMic to be launched in America.

Right now, the carmaker is cautious as supply chain problems is affecting production of its vehicles.

Tesla's other offerings

Tesla's original in-car karaoke software called "Caraoke" was introduced in 2019.

Tesla Arcade offers games like solitaire, but this is under scrutiny by the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration due to fears drivers can be distracted while the vehicle is in motion.

Elon Musk has also moved beyond electric cars and launched Tesla Tequila.

The lightning-shaped bottle sells for $250.

In 2020, it sold a limited-edition red satin short-shorts to celebrate the company's skyrocketing stock.

The short-shorts had a "S3XY" on the back, which referred to Tesla's Model S, Model 3, Model X and Model Y vehicles.

It sold for $69 but a shortage of the satin shorts had customers frustrated.

In January, Mr Musk also announced customers can use the meme-based cryptocurrency, Dogecoin, to buy some items in the Tesla shop.
